A Night at the World's Most Famous Arena

By Jack Settleman ✓ ·Madison Square Garden · New York·9.2

There is no building in sports quite like the Garden. Get there right, sit in the right seat, eat the right sandwich, and a Knicks night becomes the best crowd in the NBA. Here's exactly how to do it.

Getting in

Best ways in: walking, the subway, the LIRR, or NJ Transit. Driving would be my last choice. And when you get to the doors, come in through the 100 level gates — the merch store there has a way shorter line than the one in the MSG lobby.

If you want to slow it down, there's a Chase reserve lounge inside the arena you can book a spot in pregame, or hit Nick and Stef's, Stout, or Mustang Harry's on the way over.

Where to sit

Front rows in the 200s in the center sections beat behind the basket, even down low. The Chase Bridge and the Hyundai Bridge are both cool spots, and the Hyundai Bridge is real value. If you can get into the Delta Club, it comes with all-inclusive food and drink.

"Front rows in the 200s beat behind the basket, even down low."

What to eat

The best food in MSG is the steak sandwich outside section 107. The hamburgers from the Garden Market are the best basic food item in sports — they use a different bun that's fantastic. Carnegie Deli is another good one. Skip the pizza, it's bad in the arena. And know going in: alcoholic drinks are very expensive.

The atmosphere

Best crowd in the NBA. Be ready to stand, and the seats are tight between people. There aren't as many gimmicks as other NBA arenas, so it's not the most family-friendly, but there's still plenty of in-game entertainment like t-shirt tosses. The energy is worth it.
